Scott Sampson

February Speaker

Scott Sampson is a renowned paleontologist, author, and science communicator best known for his work in bringing natural history and earth sciences to the public. He has appeared in numerous documentaries and is widely recognized as the host of the popular PBS Kids series Dinosaur Train. In addition to his media work, Sampson has served as a research curator and museum executive, focusing on helping people build deeper connections with nature.

About the lecture

In his upcoming lecture, Sampson will explore the challenges of reconnecting children—and adults—with the natural world. Drawing from years of research in paleontology and science education, he will share insights on environmental stewardship, scientific curiosity, and how ancient life can teach us about the future of our planet.

Books by Scott Sampson

  • Dinosaur Odyssey: Fossil Threads in the Web of Life
  • How to Raise a Wild Child: The Art and Science of Falling in Love with Nature

Lecture day, time, and location

Scott Sampson's lecture will be held on the second Wednesday of February at 10:30 a.m. at William Saroyan Theatre, 700 M Street, Fresno, CA.
